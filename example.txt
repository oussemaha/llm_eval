### Clinical Interpretation:

The patient presents with a complex profile consistent with **Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS)**, characterized by:
*   **Hirsutism (2++) and Spanioménorrhée:** Classic signs of hyperandrogenism.
*   **High FSH (31 mIU/mL) and Low LH (1.5 mIU/mL):** Suggests anovulation and possible diminished ovarian reserve, common in PCOS.
*   **Obesity (BMI 38.5):** A major comorbidity in PCOS that negatively impacts fertility and increases risk of gestational diabetes.
*   **Endometrial Hypertrophy:** A common finding in PCOS due to unopposed estrogen, increasing risk of endometrial hyperplasia or cancer if not managed.

The initial treatment strategy (weight loss, metformin, Duphaston) was appropriate to address the metabolic and hormonal imbalances. The patient's success in losing 13 kg is a significant positive factor, as weight loss improves insulin sensitivity, ovulation rates, and pregnancy outcomes in PCOS.

The previous attempt with Letrozole-based IUI was not successful. While Letrozole is an effective ovulation inducer for PCOS, its success rate in unexplained infertility is variable, and in this case, the patient's endometrial factors (hypertrophy, possibly poor receptivity) may have contributed to the failure. The patient's endometrial hypertrophy, as noted in the hysteroscopy and HSG, is a key factor that may have impaired implantation.

The decision to proceed directly to IVF at the hospital is well-justified and aligns with evidence-based guidelines for PCOS and unexplained infertility. IVF bypasses the need for ovulation induction and allows for direct embryo transfer, which is particularly beneficial in cases with endometrial abnormalities or suboptimal sperm parameters.

### Evidence / Rationale:

*   **Weight Loss & PCOS:** Studies show that weight loss in women with PCOS significantly improves ovulation rates and pregnancy outcomes (RetrievalResult 10.1016/j.fertnstert.2020.06.002, 10.1016/j.fertnstert.2005.09.047).
*   **Letrozole for PCOS:** Letrozole is at least as effective as clomiphene for inducing ovulation and achieving pregnancy in PCOS (RetrievalResult 10.1016/j.fertnstert.2007.03.094). However, its use in unexplained infertility is less effective than gonadotropins (RetrievalResult 10.1016/j.fertnstert.2007.07.1325), and in this case, the patient's endometrial factors may have been the limiting factor.
*   **IVF for Unexplained Infertility:** IVF is not more effective than gonadotropins with IUI for unexplained infertility, but it is often the most effective option for couples who have failed other treatments (RetrievalResult 10.1016/j.fertnstert.2016.02.001). Given the patient's profile, IVF is the most appropriate next step.
*   **Endometrial Hypertrophy:** While not a direct contraindication to IVF, endometrial hypertrophy may be associated with poorer pregnancy outcomes and should be managed. The patient's endometrial hypertrophy may have been a contributing factor to the previous IUI failure, making IVF a more suitable option.
